Cultivating a New Perspective on Food

Food should not be cheap; it should be valued for the effort, care, and resources required to produce it. Instead of focusing on how to make food cheaper, it's time to shift the conversation toward keeping more of our hard-earned money so we can afford healthy, fresh, farm-to-table meals. Food is not inherently scarce and can be reproduced with relative ease, but the hard work of farmers, growers, and ranchers deserves fair compensation. Animals, as conscious beings, make profound sacrifices, and we should honor them by minimizing waste. When food reflects its true cost, we become more conscious of what we consume, fostering respect, gratitude, and a deeper connection to the sustenance that fuels our lives.
