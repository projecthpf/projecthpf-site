LIBERATE DEMOCRACY

A continuous, citizen-led governance system. Vote directly on issues that matter to you — or delegate your voice to someone you trust on topics they know best. Change your mind any time.

Join the Movement See How It Works
Why Now

Democracy was designed for the horse-and-buggy era.

We elect representatives every two or four years, then watch them disappear behind closed doors. Between elections, the people who are most affected by decisions have almost no say in them. Liberate Democracy fixes that — without replacing your government, your constitution, or your rights.

✕ One vote every four years

Citizens get a single, blunt instrument to express their will on thousands of decisions over half a decade. It's not enough.

✕ Policy made behind closed doors

By the time a bill reaches the floor, the real choices have already been made — by lobbyists, donors, and party leaders. Not by you.

✕ No real-time feedback loop

Representatives have no continuous mechanism to hear from constituents. They guess what voters want — and they often guess wrong.

✕ Polarization and disengagement

When communities feel unheard, they stop showing up. The loudest voices fill the void and the center collapses.

How It Works

Three ways to participate. You choose.

Liberate Democracy gives every Revolutionist three tools — and the freedom to mix & match them however they want, on every issue, in real time.

1

Propose

Have an idea for your neighborhood, city, or state? Draft it. Bring it to your local Liberation Council. If your neighbors sign on, it goes to a vote.

2

Vote

Weigh in directly on every active proposal using a Schulze-method ranked ballot — a voting system designed to find the option a community can genuinely live with.

3

Delegate

Don't have time to study every issue? Pick a neighbor, teacher, or expert you trust on that topic — hand them your vote. Take it back at any moment.

The Difference

Old democracy vs. Liberate Democracy

Liberate Democracy doesn't replace your government — it gives your community a continuous voice alongside it.

Traditional democracy

Industrial-age model
  • One vote, every 2–4 years
  • Choose between two pre-selected candidates
  • No say between elections
  • No way to delegate your voice on issues you don't know
  • Policy formation hidden from view
  • No feedback channel back to representatives

Liberate Democracy

Networked-age model
  • Continuous participation — every issue, all the time
  • Propose your own initiatives, not just react to others'
  • Vote directly on every active proposal
  • Delegate to a trusted person — and revoke any time
  • Every proposal, vote, and tally publicly visible
  • Real-time signal from neighbors to councils & lawmakers

Questions Worth Asking

You probably have some questions.

Does this replace my representative or my vote in regular elections?
No. Liberate Democracy is a parallel layer, not a replacement. You still vote in every public election. Liberation Councils give your community a continuous deliberative voice on every issue — between elections, on local matters, and on the things lawmakers never get around to. Think of it as the missing feedback loop.
What's stopping a celebrity or billionaire from collecting millions of delegations?
Three things. First: delegations are per-topic — you might delegate "housing" to your neighbor but "climate" to a scientist friend. Nobody gets a blanket vote. Second: you can revoke any delegation in one tap. Third: delegation chains and tallies are fully public, so abuse is visible. Power that's visible is power that can be checked.
How is this different from existing online petition sites?
Petition sites collect signatures and hand them to elected officials. They have no binding mechanism, no deliberation phase, and no way to delegate. Liberate Democracy is a continuous governance system — proposals are drafted, debated, ranked, and voted on by verified members of a specific community. Every step is structured and recorded.
What if I don't know enough to vote on something?
That's exactly what delegation is for. Find someone you trust who does know — an expert, a teacher, a friend who's done the reading — and hand them your vote on that topic. They vote for you until you take it back. You spend your attention only where you have real knowledge or strong feelings.
Is this just for one political party or ideology?
No. Liberate Democracy is governance infrastructure — it's the how, not the what. Any community can use it to deliberate on anything, from local ordinances to neighborhood block-party planning. The mechanism is neutral. The communities that use it are diverse.
How do you prevent fraud, bots, and astroturfing?
Every member is verified before they can vote. Identity checks happen at signup. Members can also be verified by trusted neighbors. Suspicious behavior is surfaced by a quiet-flagging system that lets members report — but never single-handedly silence — bad actors. All data is auditable.
What does it cost to participate?
Membership is $1/month — cancel any time. That covers verification, infrastructure, and keeps the lights on without selling your data or running ads. If $1 is a hardship, we have community-sponsored memberships available — just reach out.
